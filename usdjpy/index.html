For decades, borrowed Yen funded carry trades globally — courtesy of near-zero BoJ rates vs. higher yields elsewhere. That dynamic is reversing. Japan now faces decades-high sustained inflation, forcing the BoJ to raise rates (0.5% as of Sept 2025) and begin unwinding its ¥75tn ETF/REIT book. Simultaneously, the USD faces structural headwinds: eroding reserve currency status, Fed rate cuts underway, and a weaker labor market. The interest rate differential — the engine of the carry trade — is compressing from both sides. Historical precedent (2006–2011) shows USD/JPY fell 35%+ following a similar BoJ hiking cycle. The unwind is not a question of if, but when.
