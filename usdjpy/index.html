Investment Thesis

For decades, borrowed Yen funded carry trades globally — courtesy of near-zero BoJ rates vs. higher yields elsewhere. That dynamic is reversing. Japan now faces decades-high sustained inflation, forcing the BoJ to raise rates (0.5% as of Sept 2025) and begin unwinding its ¥75tn ETF/REIT book. Simultaneously, the USD faces structural headwinds: eroding reserve currency status, Fed rate cuts underway, and a weaker labor market. The interest rate differential — the engine of the carry trade — is compressing from both sides. Historical precedent (2006–2011) shows USD/JPY fell 35%+ following a similar BoJ hiking cycle.

Catalysts & Risks
Catalysts
  • BoJ Oct + Dec meetings — 2 dissenting members already eyeing 0.75%. Any hike triggers immediate JPY strengthening.
  • Fed Oct + Dec meetings — further cuts narrow the differential mechanically, forcing carry unwind.
  • BoJ ETF sales hit market — reduces domestic liquidity, supports JPY repatriation.
  • USD reserve currency erosion — EUR, GBP, CHF all strengthened vs. USD in 2025 ahead of Fed cuts.
  • US manufacturing devaluation agenda — any explicit USD weakness policy accelerates the move.
Key Risks
  • Japanese economic slowdown — BoJ could pause or reverse hikes if growth disappoints, stalling JPY appreciation.
  • Stickier US inflation — delays Fed cuts, keeps differential wide, supports USD/JPY.
  • Risk-off shock — JPY is a safe haven; a sharp global selloff could accelerate the unwind faster than anticipated.
  • BoJ communication error — any dovish signal at Oct/Dec meetings delays the catalyst timeline.
